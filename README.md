## What is PMG?

Pitmasters Grill (PMG) is a desktop pilot-intel companion for EVE Online built for the moments when local spikes and you need the useful part now.

Paste in a local list, let PMG resolve the supporting context, and get back a cleaner view of who is present, what they are tied to, and which pilots deserve a closer look.

The mission is simple:

- take a local list of pilots
- resolve useful intel quickly
- present it in a clean, readable way
- stay practical during real gameplay, not just in theory

PMG is inspired by the older spirit of community-built EVE tools: useful, shared, a little rough around the edges at first, and built because somebody cared enough to make something for other players.

This is not meant to be noise. This is meant to be fuel.

## Inspiration

Pitmasters Grill was inspired in part by **Pirates Little Helper (PLH)** and the kind of value older community-made EVE tools brought to the game.

PMG is not a re-creation of PLH, and it is not trying to replace what it was. This project exists as its own thing. But it would be dishonest not to acknowledge the spark: tools like PLH helped show what a useful community-built companion could feel like, and that inspiration helped light the fire for this project.

To the original creators and contributors who helped make those tools part of EVE's history: thank you.

## What PMG is being built to do

Pitmasters Grill is being built to help players move from raw local data to usable intel with less friction and less wasted time.

That includes:

- reading pasted local pilot lists quickly
- resolving character, corporation, and alliance context
- surfacing recent activity that actually matters
- reducing the number of browser tabs needed to answer simple questions
- helping scouts, hunters, small gangs, response fleets, and support roles get to decision-grade intel faster

PMG is not trying to drown the user in data for its own sake.

The goal is to take the raw ingredients, cut down the noise, and serve something that is fast, readable, and useful when decisions need to happen now.

---

## House Rules

PMG is being shaped around a few simple rules:

### Fast enough to matter

If intel shows up after the fight, it is garnish.

### Clear over clever

The point is to help you make a call, not admire the UI while somebody tackles your scout.

### Community-first

This is being built to share with the community, not lock away behind nonsense.

### Tools support judgment

PMG should help players think faster, not stop thinking for them.

### Keep it useful

If a feature looks flashy but slows the meal down, it probably does not belong on the plate.

## Why it will be free

PMG is planned as a **free community tool**.

No paywall. No required donation. No weird gatekeeping.

If PMG ends up being useful to you and you want to give something back, the preferred gesture is not sending money to the developer.

Instead:

* donate to a local food bank
* cook a meal for somebody
* help feed someone in your community
* share a picture or story of that act if you want to

That idea matters to this project.

Food means care. Hospitality. Looking after people. Making sure somebody is alright before anything else.

So if PMG gives you something useful, pass something good forward. That feels more in the spirit of this project than tossing a tip jar on the counter.

## Planned Feature Direction

The exact release scope is still being refined, but current direction includes:

* desktop-first workflow
* fast pilot list processing
* progressive loading where possible
* recent pilot activity context from public data sources
* corporation and alliance context
* direct links to deeper source material when needed
* local caching to reduce repeated lookups and improve speed

Some things may shift before first release. The priority is not to serve everything at once. The priority is to serve something solid, hot, and genuinely useful.
